AnyScaleSR: Integrating Semantic Preservation and Scale Control in Diffusion for High-Quality Arbitrary-Scale Image Super-Resolution

Xinning Chai, Zhengxue Cheng, Yuhong Zhang, Hengsheng Zhang, Rong Xie, Li Song,
Shanghai Jiao Tong University

Our AnyScaleSR proposes to integrate semantic preservation and scale control mechanisms into Diffusion, enhancing both fidelity and realism across different SR scales, and significantly boosting SR performance at ultra-high scaling factors.

Abstract

Although recent diffusion-based super-resolution methods have significantly improved image realism and detail generation by leveraging pre-trained Text-to-Image (T2I) priors, they struggle with arbitrary scale super-resolution due to the lack of effective SR scale control. Moreover, as the SR factor increases, it becomes challenging to maintain semantic fidelity using only low-level feature constraints. To overcome these limitations, we propose AnyScaleSR, which (1) introduces global and local scale control mechanisms to adapt the diffusion-based super-resolution process for varying SR scales, and (2) integrates a global semantic preservation mechanism to ensure high image fidelity.
Specifically, we design a global scale control mechanism that injects SR scale information into each block of the diffusion denoising network, enabling the network to adaptively extract and process features based on the SR scale. Additionally, we introduce a local scale control mechanism that dynamically refines the spatial LR image condition features according to the SR scale. For semantic preservation, we incorporate high-level text descriptions and fine-grained semantic features to apply dual-level semantic preservation by the cross-attention mechanism. Experimental results demonstrate that AnyScaleSR enhances both fidelity and realism across different SR scales, as evidenced by improvements in LPIPS, FID, DISTS, and CLIPIQA, while significantly boosting SR performance at ultra-high scaling factors such as ×24.

Semantic Preservation and Scale Control

We leverage the pretrained T2I diffusion prior, and integrate semantic preservation and scale control to dynamically generate SR scale-conditioned features for better textures and details while preserving consistent contents and semantics with those in LR images.

Overview

Our AnyScaleSR comprises two stages: Low Resolution (LR) condition preparation and conditional enhancement：
(1) Stage 1: The LR condition preparation stage coarsely upsamples the image to the target high-resolution image; while extracting semantics from the LR image.
(2) Stage 2: We generate the high-resolution (HR) image conditioned on the coarsely upsampled image, the SR scale, and the extracted LR semantics. The diffusion architecture includes a main generation branch and a condition branch. We design global and local scale control mechanisms to apply SR scale conditions, and incorporate global semantic preservation mechanisms to improve semantic fidelity.
